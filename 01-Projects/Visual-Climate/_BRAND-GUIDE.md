# Visual Climate — Brand Guide

## Brand Essence
Data-driven storytelling at the intersection of climate, development, and AI.

## Tone of Voice
- **Analytical** — always backed by data, never speculative
- **Warm** — approachable, not academic
- **Bilingual** — natural Korean-English code-switching
- **Honest** — no clickbait, no exaggeration

## Visual Identity
- (Define: color palette, typography, logo usage)
- Infographic style: clean, data-forward, UNFPA-compatible
- Thumbnail style: bold text overlay, face + data visual, high contrast

## Platform-Specific Voice

### YouTube (Korean)
- Conversational, mix of formal/informal
- Hook in first 5 seconds
- Data visualization walkthroughs
- End with actionable takeaway

### Xiaohongshu (Chinese)
- Friendly, practical, tip-focused
- Emojis used naturally
- Culturally adapted, NOT translated
- Focus on visual design tips and career insights

### LinkedIn (English)
- Professional, insight-driven
- Short paragraphs, clear structure
- Data points and lessons learned
- Development sector audience

### Newsletter
- Deep-dive analysis
- "What I shipped this month" format
- Bilingual (Korean primary, English sections)

## Content Do's and Don'ts
DO: cite sources, show process, be vulnerable about challenges
DON'T: clickbait, make unsupported claims, over-polish (authenticity matters)
