# Climate Data Literacy Engine
### Democratizing Climate Data for Negotiators, Researchers, and SIDS

---

## Slide 1: Title

**Climate Data Literacy Engine**
*AI-powered tools to make climate data accessible, understandable, and actionable*

AICA Award Application

## Slide 2: The Problem

**Climate data exists. Climate data literacy doesn't.**

- **31% of climate researchers** report significant barriers accessing
  relevant data (Nature Climate Change, 2024)
- **SIDS negotiators** enter COP with 1/10th the analytical capacity
  of developed nations
- **Misinformation** about climate data spreads 6x faster than corrections
- The gap between data availability and data usability is widening

---

## Slide 3: Who Feels This

**Climate Negotiators (especially SIDS)**
- Under-resourced delegations facing complex data-driven negotiations
- Need: rapid data interpretation, counter-narrative tools

**Researchers & Analysts**
- Drowning in datasets, struggling with cross-source synthesis
- Need: automated narrative generation, visualization

**Policy Makers**
- Making decisions based on incomplete data understanding
- Need: plain-language data briefings, misconception checks

## Slide 5: The Solution

**Discover → Understand → Question → Build → Act**

A 5-stage pipeline transforming raw climate data into actionable knowledge:

1. **Discover** — AI finds relevant datasets for your question
2. **Understand** — Data Narrator explains what the numbers mean
3. **Question** — Misconception Detector flags common errors
4. **Build** — Visualization tools create presentation-ready charts
5. **Act** — COP Advisor translates data into negotiation positions

---

## Slide 6: Module 1 — Misconception Detector

- Input: a claim about climate data (text, article, speech)
- AI cross-references against verified sources (IPCC, WMO, UNFCCC)
- Output: accuracy score + detailed fact-check + suggested correction
- Use case: pre-COP briefing preparation, media monitoring

---

## Slide 7: Module 3 — Data Narrator

- Input: a dataset or visualization
- AI generates plain-language narrative explaining trends, outliers, implications
- Output: written narrative + auto-generated visualization
- Multilingual output: English, French, Spanish, Arabic
- Use case: report drafting, presentation preparation, public communication

---

## Slide 8: Module 5 — COP Advisor

- Input: negotiation topic + country context
- AI synthesizes relevant data, precedents, comparable positions
- Output: data-backed briefing document with talking points
- Connects to historical COP decisions database
- Use case: delegation preparation, real-time negotiation support

---

## Slide 9: Proof of Concept

**Pilot scenario:** SIDS negotiator preparing for Loss & Damage discussions

- Module 1 flags 3 misconceptions in opposing party's position paper
- Module 3 generates narrative from latest sea-level data for 5 Pacific islands
- Module 5 produces briefing with comparable negotiation outcomes from COP27-29

**Result:** Delegation enters negotiation with data parity despite 1/10th the team size.

## Executive Summary

The Climate Data Literacy Engine addresses the critical gap between
climate data availability and usability. Using AI, it provides:
misconception detection, automated data narration, and COP negotiation
support — specifically designed for under-resourced delegations and
researchers facing data access barriers (31% of researchers, per
Nature Climate Change). The 5-stage pipeline (Discover → Understand →
Question → Build → Act) transforms raw data into actionable knowledge.
