## Slide 2: The Problem

**CSE programs operate in a data vacuum.**

- **130+ countries** have some form of CSE policy, but program data is
  fragmented across spreadsheets, PDFs, and email threads
- **No unified dashboard** exists for cross-country CSE program comparison
- **Opposition monitoring** is reactive — by the time anti-CSE movements
  gain traction, program teams are already behind
- Program officers spend **40%+ of reporting time** on manual data
  aggregation rather than strategic analysis

Sources: UNESCO ITGSE, UNFPA internal program data

## Slide 5: The Solution

**One URL. All CSE knowledge. Real-time.**

Three pillars:

1. **SEE** — Interactive dashboards showing CSE operations, finance,
   and policy status across 10+ countries
2. **LEARN** — Digital learning space with AI-enhanced search,
   curated resources, and best practice sharing
3. **WATCH** — Automated signal detection monitoring media and
   political discourse for CSE-related developments

## Slide 6: How It Works — SEE (Dashboard)

**CSE Country Mapping Dashboard**

- Interactive map: click any country → program status, budget,
  key indicators, UPR engagement history
- Filter by: region, funding level, program maturity, policy status
- Automated quarterly data refresh via Google Apps Script
- Exportable visualizations for reports and presentations

**Baseline Infographics**
- 10 country visual knowledge products
- Standardized format for cross-country comparison
- UNFPA brand-compliant design
- Available in English, French, Arabic

## Slide 7: How It Works — LEARN (Knowledge Hub)

**CSE Digital Learning Space**

- Document library: newsletters, event recordings, research papers
- Country profiles: one-pager per country with key metrics
- Mailing list integration: automated newsletter distribution

---

## Slide 8: How It Works — WATCH (Signal Detection)

**CSE Signal Detection System**

- Automated monitoring every 6 hours
- Keywords: "comprehensive sexuality education", "CSE policy",
  "sexuality education ban", "CSE opposition"
- Sources: news outlets, political discourse, social media
- Classification: GREEN (supportive) / YELLOW (neutral) / RED (opposition)
- Alerts: RED signals trigger immediate notification to program team
- Monthly trend reports: sentiment trajectory by country/region
